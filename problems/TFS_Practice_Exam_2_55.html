Full Solution: A pump requires 120 horsepower to transport 1,400 GPM. What percent reduction in power will be realized when the flow rate is reduced to 800 GPM. So we know from the affinity loss and you can search the reference handbook for pump affinity loss that the relationship between volume flow rate, the ratio of the new volume flow rate to the old volume flow rate, is linearly proportional to the ratio of the speed, the new speed, to the old speed. So that's one fact and then another thing that we know is that the power, BHP2, the new power, as compared to the original power, is proportional to the cube of the speed, so N2 over N1 quantity cube. And since the speed ratio is linearly proportional to the volume flow ratio, we can simply substitute Q2 over Q1 in for N2 over N1. So then we can write BHP2 over BHP1 equals Q2 over Q1 quantity cube. And then we can multiply BHP1 over to the right side. I'm assuming this power is the BHP. It wouldn't matter much if it was the electrical input power to the motor that drives the pump because we're only interested in the percent change. So let's just make the assumption that it's BHP and it will come out the same regardless, because any efficiencies there would just wash out of the solution. So we'll rearrange this and say that BHP2 equals BHP1 multiplying BHP1 over the right side. Times that ratio, Q2 over Q1 cubed and we can go right to substitute N. So the BHP originally was 120 horsepower and the new volume flow rate is going to be 800 GPM as compared to the original volume flow rate, which was 1400 GPM and that ratio is cubed. So that cuts the power all the way down to 22.4 horsepower, which is a pretty significant reduction. And then the question is, what percent reduction of power will be realized when the flow rate is reduced to 800 GPM? So what is that as a percentage of the original? We'll do 120 minus 22.4 over the original, which is 120. And that is 81 percent. Best answer choice is D.
