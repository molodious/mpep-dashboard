📹 Video Solution Walkthrough:

A cooling tower has a range of 50 in degrees and a volume flow rate of 50 gpm. Air enters a 88 degrees drive bulb and 75 degrees wet bulb and exits at 92 degrees and 75% relative humidity. Assuming no losses, what is the required volume flow rate of air? So the overall principle here is that whatever heat is rejected by the condenser water has to be absorbed into the air, that's the heat transfer that's taking place. So for the water side we can just use the sensible heat rule of thumb and we can determine the quantity of heat that was removed from the condenser water and then for the air side we'll use the mass flow rate of air times delta h and then later on we'll change that mass flow rate into a volume flow rate using the specific volume.
