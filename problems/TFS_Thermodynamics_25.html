Steam enters a 65% efficient turbine at a thousand degrees and 1200 psi and exits at 250 psi. What is the enthalpy of the exiting steam? So we have a turbine and we know entering state 1, temperature and pressure, that's fully defined. And then for the exit condition, we know only the pressure, call that state 2, and we know the overall efficiency. So let's start by finding the enthalpy and the entropy for state 1. And then hopefully we can find the enthalpy at state 2 by assuming ice and tropical expansion. And then since we know the efficiency is only 65% the actual change in enthalpy will only be 65% of what it could be. So starting with state 1, you know T1 is a thousand and P1 is 1200.
