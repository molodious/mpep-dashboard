HVAC & Refrigeration · Thermodynamics · Problem 11
Problem Statement
An outside air handling unit delivers 5000 cfm of outside air. On a winter day, the outside air is 18°F and is heated to 68°F using a hot water coil. Hot water is supplied at 165°F and returned at 140°F. What is the required hot water flow rate?
Answer Choices
A.0.4 gpm
B.3 gpm
C.22 gpm ✓
D.173 gpm
Solution
Answer: C
1. Calculate the heating load: Q = ρ × V̇ × c_p × ΔT_air. 2. Use air properties: ρ ≈ 0.075 lbm/ft³, c_p ≈ 0.24 Btu/(lbm·°F). 3. Q = 0.075 × 5000 × 60 × 0.24 × (68-18) = 0.075 × 5000 × 60 × 0.24 × 50. 4. Or use the shortcut: Q = 1.08 × CFM × ΔT = 1.08 × 5000 × 50 = 270,000 Btu/hr. 5. Set equal to water side: Q = 500 × GPM × ΔT_water → 270,000 = 500 × GPM × 25. 6. GPM = 270,000 / 12,500 = 21.6 ≈ 22 gpm → Answer C.
This is a classic HVAC energy balance problem: match the heating load (air side) to the hot water capacity (water side). Air Side — Heating Load: Using the standard HVAC shortcut: Q = 1.08 × CFM × ΔT • CFM = 5,000 • ΔT_air = 68°F - 18°F = 50°F • Q = 1.08 × 5,000 × 50 = 270,000 Btu/hr The 1.08 factor comes from: (0.075 lbm/ft³)(60 min/hr)(0.24 Btu/(lbm·°F)) = 1.08 Btu/(min·ft³·°F) Water Side — Required Flow Rate: Using the water shortcut: Q = 500 × GPM × ΔT_water • ΔT_water = 165°F - 140°F = 25°F • 270,000 = 500 × GPM × 25 • GPM = 270,000 / 12,500 = 21.6 gpm ≈ 22 gpm → Answer C. The 500 factor comes from: (8.33 lbm/gal)(60 min/hr)(1.0 Btu/(lbm·°F)) = 500 Btu/(hr·gpm·°F) These shortcut factors (1.08 for air, 500 for water) are among the most commonly used in HVAC engineering and appear repeatedly on the PE exam.

⭐ Key Takeaways:
• HVAC shortcut factors: Q_air = 1.08 × CFM × ΔT and Q_water = 500 × GPM × ΔT. Memorize these.

• Energy balance: the heating/cooling load on the air side equals the energy delivered/absorbed by the water side.

• Be careful to use the correct ΔT for each side — air ΔT ≠ water ΔT.

• This problem pattern (match air-side load to water-side capacity) is one of the most common on the HVAC PE exam.
