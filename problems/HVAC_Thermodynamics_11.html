Problem Statement

A. 0.4gpm B. 3gpm C. 22gpm D. 173gpm The air undergoes sensible heating. Use the sensible heating rule of thumb: Q =1.08CFM∆T s air The heat given up by the water can be determined with the sensible heating/cooling rule of thumb for water: Q =500GPM∆T w water The quantity of heat added to the air is supplied entirely by the hot water, therefore we can equate the two formulas: 1.08CFM∆T =500GPM∆T air water The GPM of the hot water is the only unknown. Isolate, substitute, and solve: 1.08CFM∆T 1.08(5000)(68−18) GPM = air = =21.6GPM 500∆T 500(165−140) water Note that no units need to be included when using these rules of thumb provided all input values are in the correct units in the first place i.e. CFM, GPM, and Farenheit.
