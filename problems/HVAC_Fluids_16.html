Problem
HVAC & Refrigeration  ·  Fluids  ·  Problem 16
A 1600 rpm centrifugal pump with an 8 in impeller requires 7.5 bhp when delivering 250 gpm of water at a head of 75 ft. What is the new capacity when the impeller diameter is increased to 12 in?
Answer Choices
A 250 gpm
B 305 gpm
C ✓ 375 gpm
D 560 gpm
Solution
✓ Correct Answer: C — 375 gpm
⚡ Quick Overview
  1. Recognize this is an Affinity Laws (pump similarity laws) problem — impeller diameter changes, speed stays constant.
  2. Identify the relevant affinity law for flow rate: Q scales linearly with impeller diameter (Q₂/Q₁ = D₂/D₁).
  3. Plug in the known values: D₁ = 8 in, D₂ = 12 in, Q₁ = 250 gpm.
  4. Calculate the diameter ratio: 12/8 = 1.5
  5. Multiply original flow by the ratio: Q₂ = 250 × 1.5 = 375 gpm.
  6. Select Answer C. (Note: the 7.5 bhp and 75 ft head data are extra — not needed for this specific question.)
📋 Full Solution

Step 1 — Identify the governing principle.

Centrifugal pump performance is governed by the Affinity Laws (also called Pump Similarity Laws). These laws describe how flow rate (Q), head (H), and power (P) change when either the rotational speed (N) or the impeller diameter (D) of a pump is varied — assuming the other variable is held constant.

In this problem, the pump speed is fixed at 1600 rpm and only the impeller diameter is changed from 8 in to 12 in. We therefore use the diameter-change form of the affinity laws:

Affinity Laws (speed N = constant, diameter D varies): Flow Rate: Q₂/Q₁ = D₂/D₁ Head: H₂/H₁ = (D₂/D₁)² Power: P₂/P₁ = (D₂/D₁)³

Step 2 — List the given information.

Parameter Given (State 1) New (State 2)
Speed, N 1600 rpm 1600 rpm (unchanged)
Impeller Diameter, D 8 in 12 in
Flow Rate, Q 250 gpm ? (solve for this)
Head, H 75 ft (not asked)
Power, P (bhp) 7.5 bhp (not asked)

Notice that the problem provides head (75 ft) and power (7.5 bhp). These values are distractors for this particular question — they would be used if you were asked for the new head or new power, but since the question only asks for the new capacity (flow rate), you only need the flow affinity law and the diameter ratio.

Step 3 — Apply the flow affinity law.

Q₂/Q₁ = D₂/D₁ Q₂ = Q₁ × (D₂/D₁) Q₂ = 250 gpm × (12 in / 8 in) Q₂ = 250 gpm × 1.5 Q₂ = 375 gpm

Step 4 — Verify reasonableness and select the answer.

The new impeller is 50% larger in diameter (12/8 = 1.5). The affinity law tells us flow scales proportionally with diameter at constant speed, so a 50% larger impeller produces 50% more flow: 250 × 1.5 = 375 gpm. This makes physical sense — a larger impeller sweeps more fluid per revolution.

For completeness (not required here), the new head and power would be:

H₂ = 75 ft × (12/8)² = 75 × 2.25 = 168.75 ft P₂ = 7.5 bhp × (12/8)³ = 7.5 × 3.375 = 25.3 bhp
✓ Answer: Q₂ = 375 gpm → Choice C

⭐ Key Takeaways
  • Flow scales linearly with diameter when speed is held constant — doubling the diameter doubles the flow. No exponent needed for Q.
  • Watch for distractors. PE exam pump problems often give more data than you need. Identify which affinity law applies before plugging in numbers.
  • Know the three affinity law exponents cold: Q is first power, H is squared, P is cubed — both for speed changes and diameter changes.
  • Diameter change vs. speed change: The ratio structure is the same, but be careful which variable is changing. Speed change problems use N₂/N₁; diameter change problems use D₂/D₁.

About This Problem Type

Affinity Laws (pump similarity laws) problems are a high-frequency topic on the HVAC & Refrigeration PE exam. They test your ability to quickly scale pump performance when either the impeller size or operating speed changes.

The key mental model: think of a centrifugal pump impeller as a spinning paddle. A larger paddle sweeps more water per rotation (flow scales with D), but the energy added per unit volume also increases (head scales with D²), and since both flow and head increase simultaneously, power rises steeply (P scales with D³).

On the exam, these problems are often presented with extra data (like head or power) to test whether you can identify the correct relationship for the specific quantity being asked. Always start by identifying: What is changing? What is being held constant? What is being asked?

Speed-change affinity laws work identically in structure — just substitute N₂/N₁ for D₂/D₁. Combination changes (both speed and diameter) multiply the ratios together.

Related Concepts
  • Specific Speed (Nₛ) — dimensionless parameter that characterizes pump type; remains constant for geometrically similar pumps
  • Pump Curve — system curve intersects pump curve at the operating point; changing D or N shifts the pump curve
  • NPSH (Net Positive Suction Head) — also scales with diameter change: NPSH scales as (D₂/D₁)²
  • Pump Efficiency — assumed to remain constant across affinity law calculations (geometrically similar operating point)
  • Parallel vs. Series Pumping — affinity laws apply to individual pumps; system effects change when pumps are combined
