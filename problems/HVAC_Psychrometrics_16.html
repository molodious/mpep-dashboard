Full Solution: Psychometrics problem 16. At what temperature will moisture begin to condense out of atmospheric air being cooled from 200 degrees and 10% relative humidity? Well, that's another way of asking what's the dew point, right? That's the temperature by definition at which water vapor begins to condense out of air as it's being cooled. So we know that we can find the dew point pretty easily on the psychometric chart under normal conditions. The only thing that's a bit interesting here is that we have this very high temperature situation, right? The psychometric chart normally only goes up to maybe 150 degrees or so. So instead we're going to use the high temperature psychometric chart. And as air gets warmer, it's ability to hold moisture becomes higher. And it can actually hold an incredible amount of moisture when air gets to be a very high temperature. So what will notice on the high temp psych chart is that the relative humidity curves actually shows some fairly low percentages. The most important thing is that the high temperature is not relative humidity. It's actually fairly substantial amount of water vapor per pound of dry air compared to what we're used to seeing on the normal chart. So let's take a look at that now. We have 200 degrees and 10%. So 200 degrees is here. And it's interesting to that these lines are not perfectly vertical in the high temperature chart. You get thrown off by that. It's still a linear scale. They're just not straight up and down. And if you zoom in, you'll see that the percentages are some of these lines. It's not 10 to 20, 30 all the way up, right? It's 2% 4% 6% and so on. And it does get up to 80 and 90% here, but it's like the whole shape of the chart kind of changes when you get up into this high temperature range. So where is 10% humidity? That's this line here. So we got to ride that all the way up to here. That is the 10% crossing. So at that point, we can find the corresponding dew point by going horizontally to the left. And that looks like it's about halfway between 100 and 110. A little bit closer to 110. I'm going to say about 160 degrees. So let's check the answer choices. There's a choice for 107. That's probably the one. Answer choice B is the best.
