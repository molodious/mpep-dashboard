Well, normally this problem would be pretty easy because density is the reciprocal of specific volume, so we could just use the psychometric chart to look up the specific volume, and then take the inverse, and we would be done. However, the psychometric chart is generally based on sea level, and there is a psychometric chart in the reference handbook for 5,000 feet above sea level, but for an odd elevation like this 6,500 feet, there is no psychometric chart, so we have to do an elevation correction.
