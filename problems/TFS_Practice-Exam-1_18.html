100,000 pounds per hour of steam at 5 psi g with a quality of 0.9 flows through a pipe with an inside diameter of 20 inches. What is the velocity? So in order to find the velocity, let's bring up a couple of pretty well-known equations that we've used many times. We know the definition of the mass flow rate is the density times the volume flow rate. So hold that thought for a moment and we also know that the volume flow rate is the velocity times the area. So if we put these together and substitute it for the volume flow rate, we can say that mass flow rate equals density times velocity times the area. And then we can rearrange this to get the velocity by itself and save velocity equals mass flow rate divided by density and area.
