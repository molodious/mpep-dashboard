Q: If the two mixing streams had different pressures, how would you determine the pressure of the mixed stream?
A: If the final mixture is saturated, you can use energy balance to find H3, then look it up directly in the saturated steam table to find pressure—saturation alone gives you enough information. If the result is superheated, you'd need additional information beyond just enthalpy, and would need to apply energy balance differently, potentially using properties like mass flow rates and temperature changes.
