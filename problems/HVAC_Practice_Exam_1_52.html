Problem Statement
The pressure drop through a chilled water coil at the design ow rate of 25gpm is 8psi. During normal operation, the actual ow rate is 4gpm. What is the pressure drop in normal operation?
Answer Choices
A.0.2psi
B.1.3psi
C.4.1psi
D.8.0psi

Full Solution: A pump is driven by a four-pole synchronous motor. What is the nominal rotational speed ignores slip? So what is a synchronous motor? A synchronous motor is an AC motor which means it's powered by alternating current electricity and it creates a rotating magnetic field in the stator which is the outside part of the motor and that's what drives the rotation of the rotor and what's important about a synchronous motor is that it does that in step with the oscillations in the electrical current. So the speed of a synchronous motor is always in direct relationship with in fact it's an integer multiple of the frequency of the electrical current. So in the United States that's always going to be 60 and actually all of these answer choices are multiples of 60 and 50 so even if you are in a part of the world that has 50 Hertz frequency these would all still be applicable but in any case in the reference handbook if you search for synchronous speed motor you'll find a table in there which allows us to determine the RPM directly from the number of poles in the motor. So if you have more poles and the number of magnetic poles on the stator and rotor will always correspond for any motor and depending on how many poles there are that'll determine what the rotational speed's going to be and if you make that search again that synchronous speed motor and find the table all we have to do is look up in that table for four poles and we find out that the RPM is going to be 1800 and the best answer choice is B.
