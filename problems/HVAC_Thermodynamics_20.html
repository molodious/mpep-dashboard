📹 Video Solution Walkthrough:

Thermo 20, a heat pump with a 3KW compressor, has a heating capacity of 50,000 BT's per hour, and a cooling capacity of 40,000 BT's per hour. What is the COP when the unit is operated in heating mode? The heating capacity is greater than the cooling capacity for a heat pump. Why is that? Well, because the compressor produces heat and that heat has to be rejected. If your goal is to do heating as with a heat pump operated in heating mode, then the heat produced by the compressor is useful. But if your goal is to do cooling, then the heat produced by the compressor is a drag on the system. And that's why the COP for a heat pump is always greater than the COP for the same device being operated as an air conditioner.
