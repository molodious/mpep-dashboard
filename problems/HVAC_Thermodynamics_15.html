Problem 15 — Carnot Heat Pump COP from Refrigerant Pressures
Thermodynamics • Carnot Cycle • Heat Pump • R-22 Refrigerant
In a Carnot heat pump, R-22 evaporates at 31 psia and condenses at 211 psia. What is the coefficient of performance?
A. 0.9
B. 4.3
C. 5.1 ✓
D. 6.8
✓ Answer: C — 5.1
1. Identify saturation temperatures from R-22 tables. • At 31 psia (evaporator): T_sat ≈ −11°F • At 211 psia (condenser): T_sat ≈ 100°F 2. Convert to absolute temperature (Rankine). • T_L = −11 + 459.67 ≈ 448.7 R • T_H = 100 + 459.67 ≈ 559.7 R 3. Apply the Carnot heat pump COP formula. COP_HP = T_H / (T_H − T_L) COP_HP = 559.7 / (559.7 − 448.7) COP_HP = 559.7 / 111 COP_HP ≈ 5.04 4. Select the closest answer. 5.04 ≈ 5.1 → Answer C ✓ 5. Sanity check. COP_HP > 1 always for a heat pump — confirmed. ✓
Background A Carnot heat pump is the ideal, reversible heat pump operating between two fixed temperature reservoirs. Its COP depends only on those reservoir temperatures — not on the working fluid properties beyond what the saturation temperatures tell us. Step 1 — Look up R-22 Saturation Temperatures Given pressures directly correspond to saturation (two-phase) conditions, so the refrigerant temperature equals the saturation temperature at each pressure. From standard R-22 saturation tables (pressure-based): • 31 psia → T_sat ≈ −11°F (Interpolating between −20°F @ 24.86 psia and 0°F @ 38.73 psia: fraction = (31 − 24.86)/(38.73 − 24.86) = 6.14/13.87 = 0.443 T = −20 + 0.443 × 20 = −11.1°F) • 211 psia → T_sat ≈ 100°F (R-22 saturation at 100°F ≈ 210.6 psia — essentially exact) Step 2 — Convert to Rankine Absolute temperature is required for Carnot calculations. T_L = −11.1 + 459.67 = 448.6 R (evaporator / low side) T_H = 100.0 + 459.67 = 559.7 R (condenser / high side) Step 3 — Carnot Heat Pump COP Formula For a Carnot heat pump (which delivers heat to the high-temperature reservoir): COP_HP = T_H / (T_H − T_L) COP_HP = 559.7 / (559.7 − 448.6) = 559.7 / 111.1 = 5.04 Step 4 — Match to Answer Choices 5.04 rounds to 5.1 → Answer C ✓ Wrong Answer Analysis • A (0.9): Impossible for any heat pump — COP_HP must be > 1 by definition. This might result from inverting the formula or using temperature differences incorrectly. • B (4.3): Would result from using incorrect saturation temperatures (e.g., using −20°F and 100°F, giving 560/(560−440) = 4.67 — still not 4.3). • D (6.8): Would require a much smaller temperature difference between reservoirs; possibly from confusing psia with kPa or using wrong table values. Key Formula Distinction COP_HP = T_H / (T_H − T_L) ← heat pump (delivers heat) COP_ref = T_L / (T_H − T_L) ← refrigerator (removes heat) Note: COP_HP = COP_ref + 1 Don't confuse these on the exam! The problem says "heat pump" → use T_H in the numerator.
📌 Key Takeaways
  • R-22 saturation pressures: look up T_sat from refrigerant tables — pressures define the operating temperatures.
  • Always convert °F to Rankine (add 459.67) before applying any Carnot formula.
  • Carnot heat pump: COPHP = TH / (TH − TL) — high-side temp in the numerator.
  • COPHP is always greater than 1; if your answer is below 1, you've used the wrong formula or wrong temperature scale.
