Q: Where in the NCEES handbook can you find the equation QL = m·Δh for refrigeration cycles?
A: It's not written exactly that way in the handbook, but you derive it from understanding the refrigeration cycle diagram—specifically the p-h diagram showing states 1-4. The heat absorbed in the evaporator is Q = m(h₁ - h₄), which you learn by studying the cycle diagram and energy balances, not memorization.

OH 92: HVAC: Thermo Module #19
Q: How do we derive the equation for volume flow rate of refrigerant in a refrigeration cycle?
A: There's no single equation in the reference handbook—you derive it from first principles. Volume flow rate equals mass flow rate times specific volume at that state (V̇ = ṁ × v₁), and you divide by the cooling capacity (Q) in Btu/hr to express it per ton of refrigeration. The key insight is that mass flow rate is constant around the cycle, but volume flow rate changes because density varies at different states.
