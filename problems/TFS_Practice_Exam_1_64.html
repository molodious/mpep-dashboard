Full Solution: A pump designed to operate at 100 GPM and 50 feet of head is determined to be oversized. The pump impeller diameter is decreased by 20%. What is the resulting pressure developed by the pump after this change? The pump originally developed 50 feet of head. Now if we're going to put a smaller impeller and place of the previous one, then it's going to be less. The question is how much less. It's not linear, so it's not going to be exactly 80% of 50. It's going to be less than that because the head pressure developed by the pump goes with the square of the diameter. Much in the same way that it goes with the square of the speed or the square of the volume flow rate. But it's generally not a good idea with these affinity a lot of problems to try to move two variables at once. So we tend not to change the speed and the diameter at one time or either changing one or the other. And if you want to refresh our on what these affinity law formulas are, you can search the reference number for pump affinity laws. And you'll find two columns there, one for speed change and one for impeller diameter change. And that's the one we're interested in here. So we can say that if we use the subscript 1 for the original case and 2 for the new case, head 2 overhead 1 equals diameter 2 over diameter 1 squared. So the diameter at state 2 is 80% of the diameter at state 1. And then if we want to have that as a ratio, we can divide by d1 on both sides and get 0.8.
