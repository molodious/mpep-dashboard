Problem Statement
A plant room contains a generator with a noise level of 95dBA, an air compressor with a noise level of 88dBA, and a pump with a noise level of 77dBA. What is the overall noise level?
Answer Choices
A.87dB
B.95dB
C.96dB
D.98dB
Solution

Full Solution: A plant room contains a generator with a noise level of 95 DBA, an air compressor with a noise level of 88 DBA, and a pump with a noise level of 77 DBA. What is the overall noise level? What we found out in the beginning of the supporting topics chapter that there's a table on the reference handbook which you can find by searching, combining two sound levels, where depending on how far apart the sound levels are for two different devices, we can add one, two or three decibels to the higher of the two to get the result. So for example, if you have two devices with the same sound level to find the combined sound level we add three decibels. In this case, we're just going to focus on the two higher ones because when the difference is 10 decibels or more, that adds nothing to the result. So basically if we have a 95 decibel device and an 88 decibel device, then a 77 decibel device is far too quiet to contribute anything at all. So now we're focused on the difference between these two 95 decibels minus 88 decibels is a difference of seven. And when the difference is anywhere between five and nine, we add one decibel to the higher of the two of course, adding it to the lower would mean that the combined sound level would be lower than one of the two so that would make any sense. So the difference is seven we're going to be adding one to 95. And the combined sound level is 96 decibels, which is answer choice C.
