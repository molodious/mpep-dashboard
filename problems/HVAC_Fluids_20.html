A boiler feed pump circulates 30 gpm against 30 ft of head. A booster pump in series is rated for 5 ft of head at 30 gpm. What is the combined total discharge head for this arrangement?
Answer: D — 35 ft
A. 5 ft
B. 25 ft
C. 30 ft
D. 35 ft ✓
1. Identify the pump configuration: two pumps arranged in series. 2. Recall the rule for pumps in series: total head = sum of individual heads; flow rate stays the same. 3. The boiler feed pump contributes 30 ft of head at 30 gpm. 4. The booster pump contributes 5 ft of head at the same 30 gpm. 5. Add the heads: 30 ft + 5 ft = 35 ft total discharge head. 6. Flow rate remains 30 gpm — series pumps do not change flow. → Answer: D
This is a conceptual problem about pump configurations. The key principle to know: • Pumps in SERIES → Heads ADD, flow rate stays the same • Pumps in PARALLEL → Flow rates ADD, head stays the same The boiler feed pump provides 30 ft of head at a flow rate of 30 gpm. The booster pump is connected downstream in series, adding an additional 5 ft of head — still at the same 30 gpm flow rate. Total Discharge Head = 30 ft + 5 ft = 35 ft Flow Rate = 30 gpm (unchanged) Answer: D — 35 ft. If these pumps were in parallel instead, you would keep the head at 30 ft (or find a combined curve) but add the flow contributions — a completely different scenario. Don't mix up the two configurations on exam day.
📹 Video Synthesis
  • Pumps in series act like batteries in series — voltages (heads) add up while current (flow) stays constant.
  • The booster pump's sole purpose is to add extra head to overcome additional system resistance.
  • This is a quick conceptual problem — no calculation beyond simple addition, but the concept is critical.
  • Know the parallel vs. series distinction cold: series adds head, parallel adds flow.
  • On the exam, watch for trick answers that subtract (25 ft) or only list one pump's contribution.
⭐ Key Takeaways
  • Series pumps add head while maintaining the same flow rate — think of it like stacking pressure boosters.
  • Parallel pumps add flow while maintaining the same head — think of it like opening more lanes on a highway.
  • This concept appears repeatedly across fluids problems and is a near-guaranteed exam topic.
  • Quick conceptual problems like this are free points if you know the fundamentals — don't overthink them.
