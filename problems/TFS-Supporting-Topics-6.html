Q: Where does the angular frequency formula with GC come from, and why isn't it in the handbook? I found ω = √(K/M) but the formula I need is ω = √(K·GC/M).
A: That GC factor is an unfortunate necessity in U.S. customary units due to the distinction between pound-force and pound-mass; in SI units the formula works directly without it. The handbook doesn't show it because it's a unit conversion artifact—when you work through the dimensional analysis in US customary units, you have to insert GC (32.2 lbm·ft/lbf·s²) to make the units cancel properly, unlike SI where everything naturally works out.
