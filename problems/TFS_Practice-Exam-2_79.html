15,000 pounds per hour of air enters a gas turbine at 800 degrees and 70 PSIA. The turbine produces 300 horsepower of network and the air exits the turbine at 14.7 PSIA. What is the efficiency of the turbine? So efficiency is what we get out over what we put in and what we're getting out is 300 horsepower that's being produced by the turbine. What we're putting in could be expressed as an M. Delta H or an M.CP Delta T. In this case, we know the mass flow rate and we know the entering temperature. We don't know the leaving temperature but we can envision what that ideal leaving temperature would be if it was a constant entropy process and then that would give us our input energy and that would allow us to find the efficiency.
