Full Solution: A 95% efficient 3 phase motor draws 50 amps at 480 volts with a power factor of 0.8. The motor drives the pump which distributes 300gpm of water with a differential pressure of 150 psi. What is the efficiency of the pump? On general the efficiency of the pump is the hydraulic horsepower or WHP water horsepower divided by the brake horsepower that's driving the pump. In this case we know the volume flow rate and the delta P so we can calculate the hydraulic horsepower WHP and we also know the current and the voltage and the power factor for a 3 phase motor we can calculate the input power to the motor in KW and then using the efficiency we can find the brake horsepower produced by that motor. If we were just given the brake horsepower of the motor directly that would make this problem much easier but we're not so we're going to use some of this data about the power to figure out how much power is actually coming out of that motor and that's what's driving our pump. So let's start with WHP the water horsepower. A lot of times we use the version of this equation that is QH the head added by the pump and phi over 3960. This is an equally common equation which says Q delta P over 1714 and what you'll notice is that if you had the head added by the pump it would be 2.31 times the delta P and PSI and that's why the constant that it's being divided by if you multiply that by 2.31 you get the 3960 that we normally use. So these equations are basically the same thing and that's why you can comfortably use either one. So since we have PSI this is definitely the way to go. 300 GPM times 150 PSI divided by 1714 and that gives us 26.25 horsepower. So that's our numerator and then for the denominator we can search the reference handbook for power for different motor phases and there's a table there that will give us the power in KW for three phase motor based on the current voltage and power factor which is exactly what we need and this isn't really so much a rule of thumb formula so I'll write all the units in as we go.
