📹 Video Solution Walkthrough:

50 pounds per second of 60 degree ideal air with constant specific heats is compressed with a 70% efficient compressor. The compression ratio is 8. What is the power required to drive the compressor? In order to find the power we would normally do an m dot delta h. In this case we can do an m cp delta t because we know that it's ideal air with constant specific heats. You can actually search the reference handbook for the phrase ideal gas with constant specific heats and you'll see that the delta h can be replaced by cp delta t for that exact reason. However, what should be the delta t? We only know the temperature of the entering air. We need to know the temperature of the leaving air and it's not 100% efficient so we cannot assume an isentropic process.
⭐ Key Takeaways:
• At this point I no longer look at equations when it comes to figuring out where to put efficiency I always think about it as what do you get out as a percentage of what you put in.
• I always insist on using intuition for that step of the process or at least checking the equation at that step in the process.
• And the last thing we want to do is convert that value to horsepower.
