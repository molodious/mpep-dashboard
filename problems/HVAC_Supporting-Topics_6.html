A 5,000-pound machine rotates at 1,800 rpm and is mounted on vibration isolators with a combined stiffness of 40,000-pound force per inch. In parallel with the springs, a damper has been included. The damping ratio is 0.3. An unbalanced force of 2,000-pound force is caused by the machine. What is the maximum force transmitted through the base? We're going to find the vibration transmissibility, which is the ratio of the transmitted force to the unbalanced force. But before we can do that, we need to dig into the frequencies. So we want to know two things. We want to know the natural frequency of this setup with the springs as it stands. And we also want to know the forcing frequency, which is driven by this rotational motion of 1800 rpm, because there's probably some motor driving mass.
