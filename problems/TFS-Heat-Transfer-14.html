OH 115: TFS: Heat Transfer #14
Q: Why can't we use ΔH = CP·ΔT to find the enthalpy change between superheated steam and subcooled water, even though we used it for the initial water enthalpy?
A: Because you're trying to bridge two different phases (liquid and vapor), and the formula only works for sensible heating within a single phase. When you cross a phase boundary, you have latent heat involved—a horizontal line on the T-H diagram where temperature stays constant while enthalpy changes dramatically—which CP·ΔT can't capture.
