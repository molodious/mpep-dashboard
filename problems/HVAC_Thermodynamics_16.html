Problem 16

A refrigerator draws 800 W of power and absorbs 4000 Btu/hr from the internal volume. What is the coefficient of performance?

Answer Choices
A
0.7
B
1.5 ✓
C
1.7
D
5.0
Quick Overview

This problem asks you to find the Coefficient of Performance (COP) of a refrigerator given its power consumption and the rate at which it removes heat from the refrigerated space. The COP for a refrigerator is the ratio of the heat absorbed from the cold space to the net work input — it tells you how efficiently the refrigerator moves heat per unit of work consumed. The key challenge here is ensuring both quantities are in the same units before dividing.

Full Solution
Step 1 — Identify Given Information
W_in = 800 W (work input / power draw) Q_L = 4000 Btu/hr (heat absorbed from cold space) These two quantities are in different unit systems, so we must convert before calculating.
Step 2 — Convert Work Input to Btu/hr
Use the conversion factor: 1 W = 3.412 Btu/hr W_in = 800 W × 3.412 Btu/hr per W = 2729.6 Btu/hr Now both Q_L and W_in are in Btu/hr.
Step 3 — Apply the COP Formula for a Refrigerator
For a refrigerator, the COP is defined as the useful output (heat removed from cold space) divided by the required work input: COP_refrig = Q_L / W_in where Q_L is heat absorbed from the cold (low-temperature) reservoir and W_in is the net work input to the cycle.
Step 4 — Calculate COP
Substituting the values (both in Btu/hr): COP = Q_L / W_in = 4000 Btu/hr ÷ 2729.6 Btu/hr = 1.466… ≈ 1.5
Step 5 — Select the Answer
COP ≈ 1.47, which rounds to 1.5.
✓ Answer: B) 1.5
Concept Deep-Dive
What COP Means Physically
The COP tells you how many units of cooling you get per unit of work you put in. A COP of 1.5 means: for every 1 Btu of electrical work consumed, the refrigerator pumps 1.5 Btu out of the cold space. Unlike thermal efficiency (which is always ≤ 1), COP can exceed 1 because you're moving heat — not converting it.
Refrigerator COP vs. Heat Pump COP
Both devices use the same vapor-compression cycle, but the useful output differs:
  • Refrigerator: COPR = QL / Win — valued for cooling
  • Heat Pump: COPHP = QH / Win — valued for heating
  • Relationship: COPHP = COPR + 1 (always)
Choosing the wrong formula is one of the most common exam mistakes on this topic.
Common Pitfalls
  • Forgetting to convert units — mixing W and Btu/hr gives a garbage answer (e.g., 4000/800 = 5.0, which is choice D, a classic trap).
  • Using QH instead of QL in the refrigerator COP formula.
  • Confusing COP with efficiency — efficiency is dimensionless and ≤ 1; COP has no such upper limit.
  • Using the Carnot COP formula when actual operating data is given — always use given values first.
Key Takeaways
  • Refrigerator COP = QL / Win — use the heat removed from the cold space, not the heat rejected to the hot side.
  • Unit conversion is mandatory: 1 W = 3.412 Btu/hr. If you skip this step, you'll land on the distractor answer (5.0).
  • COP can be > 1 for refrigerators and heat pumps — this is normal and expected; you're moving heat, not generating it from scratch.
  • COPHP = COPR + 1 — heat pump COP is always exactly 1 greater than refrigerator COP for the same cycle.
  • Double-check your formula — the exam will offer distractor choices that match incorrect formula results; label your Q's clearly.
