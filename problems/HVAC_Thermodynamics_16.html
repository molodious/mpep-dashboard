Problem 16 — Refrigerator Coefficient of Performance
Thermodynamics • COP • Unit Conversion • Refrigeration Cycles
A refrigerator draws 800 W of power and absorbs 4000 Btu/hr from the internal volume. What is the coefficient of performance?
A. 0.7
B. 1.5 ✓
C. 1.7
D. 5.0
✓ Answer: B — 1.5
1. Identify the formula: COP_refrigerator = Q_L / W_net where Q_L = heat absorbed from the cold space, W_net = work input 2. Identify given values: W_net = 800 W Q_L = 4000 Btu/hr 3. Convert units so both are in the same system. Convert W_net to Btu/hr: 800 W × 3.412 Btu/hr per W = 2,729.6 Btu/hr 4. Calculate COP: COP = Q_L / W_net = 4000 / 2729.6 ≈ 1.47 5. Select the closest answer: 1.47 ≈ 1.5 → Answer B
━━━ BACKGROUND ━━━ The Coefficient of Performance (COP) is a dimensionless efficiency metric for refrigerators and heat pumps. Unlike thermal efficiency (which is always less than 1), COP can be greater than 1 — a COP of 1.5 means the system moves 1.5 units of heat energy for every 1 unit of work input. For a refrigerator (or air conditioner): COP_ref = Q_L / W_net Where: Q_L = rate of heat removal from the cold reservoir (the "useful effect") W_net = net work input (power consumed) For a heat pump operating on the same cycle: COP_HP = Q_H / W_net = COP_ref + 1 ━━━ STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION ━━━ Step 1 — Write the COP formula: COP = Q_L / W_net Step 2 — List known values: Q_L = 4000 Btu/hr (heat absorbed from refrigerator interior) W_net = 800 W (electrical power drawn) Step 3 — Unit conversion (critical step!): Q_L and W_net must be in the same units before dividing. Option A: Convert W_net from watts to Btu/hr 1 W = 3.412 Btu/hr 800 W × 3.412 = 2,729.6 Btu/hr Option B: Convert Q_L from Btu/hr to watts 1 Btu/hr = 0.29307 W 4000 Btu/hr × 0.29307 = 1,172.3 W Step 4 — Calculate COP (using Option A): COP = 4000 Btu/hr / 2729.6 Btu/hr = 1.466 Verify using Option B: COP = 1172.3 W / 800 W = 1.465 ✓ (consistent) Step 5 — Select the answer: COP ≈ 1.47 → closest choice is B (1.5) ━━━ WRONG ANSWER ANALYSIS ━━━ A. 0.7 — This would result if you accidentally divided W_net by Q_L (inverted the formula): 800/1172.3 ≈ 0.68. Watch the formula direction — COP = Q_L/W, not W/Q_L. C. 1.7 — Close but not correct. Likely results from a slightly off conversion factor or rounding error in intermediate steps. D. 5.0 — This might appear if no unit conversion is done and values are used directly: 4000/800 = 5.0. This is the most common trap — you MUST convert to consistent units before calculating. ━━━ KEY FORMULA NOTES ━━━ Unit conversion factors to memorize: 1 W = 3.412 Btu/hr 1 Btu/hr = 0.29307 W 1 ton of refrigeration = 12,000 Btu/hr = 3.517 kW COP relationships: COP_ref = Q_L / W_net COP_HP = Q_H / W_net = COP_ref + 1 COP_Carnot_ref = T_L / (T_H - T_L) [temperatures in absolute scale, R or K]
📌 Key Takeaways
  • COP formula for a refrigerator: COP = Q_L / W_net — heat removed divided by work input.
  • Unit conversion is mandatory: Q_L and W_net must be in the same units. The most common wrong answer (5.0) comes from skipping this step.
  • Key conversion: 1 W = 3.412 Btu/hr. Memorize this — it appears constantly in HVAC problems.
  • COP > 1 is normal and expected for refrigerators — it means you're moving more heat than the work you put in, which is thermodynamically valid (you're not creating energy, just pumping it).
  • COP_HP = COP_ref + 1 — if this were a heat pump, COP = 2.47. Know both relationships.
