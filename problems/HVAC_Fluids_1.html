Problem
HVAC & Refrigeration · Fluids · Problem 1
A centrifugal pump consumes 200 kW when operating at 1800 rpm. What is the electrical power demand when the pump speed is reduced to 1200 rpm? Assume 100% motor efficiency.
Answer Choices
A 60 kW
B 59 kW  ✓
C 66 kW
D 89 kW
Answer: B — 59 kW
Quick Overview
  1. Identify the applicable law: Pump Affinity Laws (also called Fan Laws) govern centrifugal machines.
  2. Locate the power–speed relationship: power scales with the cube of the speed ratio. P₂ / P₁ = (N₂ / N₁)³
  3. Compute the speed ratio and cube it: (N₂ / N₁) = 1200 / 1800 = 2/3 (2/3)³ = 8/27 ≈ 0.2963
  4. Apply the ratio to the original power: P₂ = 200 kW × (8/27) = 200 × 0.2963 ≈ 59.26 kW
  5. Since motor efficiency = 100%, electrical power = shaft power. No additional conversion needed.
  6. Round to appropriate precision: ≈ 59 kW → Answer B
📝 Unit note: The pump's rated power is given in kW. Applying the affinity law ratio directly in kW yields the answer cleanly. If the original power were given in hp, you would first apply the affinity ratio (getting the new shaft power in hp), then convert: 1 hp = 0.7457 kW. Always track units through the calculation.

Full Solution

Centrifugal pumps (and fans) follow the Affinity Laws, a set of dimensionless scaling relationships derived from fluid dynamic similarity. These laws describe how flow rate, head (pressure rise), and power change when the rotational speed of a geometrically similar machine changes.

The three core affinity law relationships are:

Flow rate: Q₂/Q₁ = N₂/N₁ (linear with speed) Head: H₂/H₁ = (N₂/N₁)² (square of speed ratio) Power: P₂/P₁ = (N₂/N₁)³ (cube of speed ratio)

This problem involves only the power relationship. Given:

P₁ = 200 kW at N₁ = 1800 rpm N₂ = 1200 rpm, motor efficiency η = 100%

Apply the power affinity law:

P₂ = P₁ × (N₂/N₁)³ P₂ = 200 kW × (1200/1800)³ P₂ = 200 kW × (2/3)³ P₂ = 200 kW × (8/27) P₂ = 200 × 0.29630 ≈ 59.26 kW

Because 100% motor efficiency is assumed, every watt of electrical input from the power grid is converted into shaft power — there are no losses. Therefore:

Electrical demand = Shaft power = 59.26 kW ≈ 59 kW

Answer: B — 59 kW.

The significance of the cubic relationship is dramatic: reducing speed by just one-third (from 1800 to 1200 rpm, a 33% reduction) cuts power consumption to only about 30% of the original — from 200 kW down to ~59 kW. This is the fundamental reason variable-frequency drives (VFDs) are so effective for energy savings in HVAC systems with variable load profiles.

⚡ Key Takeaways
  • The Pump Affinity Laws are essential for any problem involving speed changes in centrifugal pumps, fans, or blowers.
  • Power scales with the cube of the speed ratio — small speed reductions yield large power savings (the cubic relationship is not intuitive until you work it!).
  • When motor efficiency is stated as 100%, shaft power equals electrical power demand — no additional efficiency factor is needed.
  • Always confirm the units of the given power before applying any conversion; applying the affinity ratio first and then converting units avoids compounding errors.
