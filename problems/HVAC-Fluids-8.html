Q: What exactly qualifies as 'low pressure' for the air at low pressure tables, given that 50 psi seems higher than atmospheric pressure (14.7 psi)?
A: There's no exact threshold, but 'low pressure' typically applies to incompressible flow conditions—basically anything that's not approaching supersonic speeds or rocket-level velocities. Even tens or hundreds of psi can be considered low pressure; the real cutoff is when you transition from incompressible to compressible flow dynamics, which requires much higher pressures and velocities.
