Q: The student used isentropic efficiency with temperatures (finding T2s via isentropic assumption and steam tables, then using the efficiency formula to find actual T2 and its corresponding enthalpy) instead of enthalpies directly, and got 131 as their final answer — they want to know why this approach is incorrect.
A: Dan explains that substituting temperatures for enthalpies in the isentropic efficiency equation is only valid for ideal gases with constant specific heats, because only then does η = (T_i - T_e)/(T_i - T_s) hold true (since the cp terms cancel). Since the problem involves steam and does not state ideal gas with constant specific heats, the student must use the universally true enthalpy-based form of the efficiency equation rather than the temperature-based shortcut.
