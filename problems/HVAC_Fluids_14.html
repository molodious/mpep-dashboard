Problem Statement
10,000gal of fuel oil with a speci(cid:28)c gravity of 0.86 is delivered to an hr emergency generator by a 90% e(cid:30)cient pump. The pressure gauge near the pump inlet reads −2.0inHg. The pressure gauge on the discharge side of the pump reads 30psig and is located 10ft above the pump. What is the input power to the pump?
Answer Choices
A.1.6hp
B.3.2hp
C.3.4hp
D.3.8hp ✓
Solution
Answer: D
This problem combines several fluids concepts: unit conversion, Bernoulli equation for a non-water fluid, and pump power. Flow rate: Q = 10,000 gal/hr × (1 hr/60 min) = 166.67 GPM. Inlet pressure (P₁): Given as −2.0 inches of mercury (gauge, vacuum). Convert: −2.0 inHg × (13.6 inH₂O / 1 inHg) = −27.2 inH₂O −27.2 inH₂O × (1 ft / 12 in) = −2.267 ftH₂O −2.267 ftH₂O × (1 PSI / 2.31 ftH₂O) = −0.98 PSI (gauge) Discharge pressure (P₂): 30 psig at 10 ft above pump. The 10 ft column is fuel oil, not water: 10 ft fuel × SG(0.86) = 8.6 ft equivalent water 8.6 ftH₂O / 2.31 = 3.72 PSI P₂ = 30 + 3.72 = 33.72 PSI (gauge) ΔP = P₂ − P₁ = 33.72 − (−0.98) = 34.7 PSI Hydraulic horsepower: WHP = Q × ΔP / 1714 = 166.67 × 34.7 / 1714 = 3.37 hp. (This formula works for any fluid when ΔP is in PSI — no SG needed.) Break horsepower: BHP = WHP / η_pump = 3.37 / 0.90 = 3.74 hp → Answer D. Common mistake: dividing by 3960 instead of 1714 (3960 is for head in feet of water), or multiplying by efficiency instead of dividing (which gives 3.0 hp → wrong answer).
