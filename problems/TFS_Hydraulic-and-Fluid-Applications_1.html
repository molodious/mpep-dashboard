📹 Video Solution Walkthrough:

A 10-horse power pump with 90% efficiency transports water to an elevation of 200 feet above the pump. What volume of water is transported in a one-hour period? So the way we want to think about this problem is we have a pump which is using some power to do some work to increase the elevation of some water, which in so doing increases its potential energy by virtue of it being at a higher elevation. Just like if you do work to a spring, you compress it by applying a force over a distance and then that potential energy is stored in the spring.

• It's the same here except that spring energy is stored as 1 1,5kx squared and potential energy is going to be stored using a different formula, which can be found in the reference handbook by searching potential energy, but you'll probably remember it just from basic physics.
• And if we're working in imperial units, which we are here, we also need to divide by gc, which is important to remember.
