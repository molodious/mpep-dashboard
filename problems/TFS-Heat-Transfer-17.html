Q: How is the tolerance of ±15 achieved in this run-around coil problem? Did I miss something or is this a standard value I should know?
A: The ±15 isn't a tolerance—it's derived from the energy balance on the coils. Using the sensible heat rule (500 GPM × ΔT for water), we calculate the heat extracted from the leaving air, which tells us the water's ΔT across each coil is 30°. The temperature range of the loop is then the difference between the two extremes (35°F to 65°F), which gives us the 30° delta and eliminates wrong answers.
