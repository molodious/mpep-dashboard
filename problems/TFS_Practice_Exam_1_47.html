Problem Statement
The ow of a centrifugal pump developing 150ft of head is reduced from 500gpm to 300gpm. After the speed reduction, what is the head added by the pump?
Answer Choices
A.32ft
B.54ft
C.90ft
D.250ft

Full Solution: The flow of a centrifugal pump developing 150 feet of head is reduced from 500 GPM to 300 GPM. After the speed reduction, what is the head added by the pump? So for a refresher on this, you can search the reference handbook for pump affinity laws. And what we find there is that the relationship between volume flow rate and speed is linear. So to spell that out, we can say that speed to as compared to speed 1, where 1 is the original condition and 2 is after the change, the reduction in speed. It's going to be equal to Q2 over Q1. So volume and speed are linear. And we can actually quantify that since we know the new volume is 300 GPM as compared to the old volume, which is 500 GPM, that's going to equal 0.6. So how does that allow us to find the new head? If the original head was 150 feet, well, there's another affinity law there. And they show it as being based on a speed change. You're welcome to substitute the ratio of the volume flow rates directly in for speeds since this relationship always holds true. But if you prefer to work with speeds, we can now say that we've quantified the ratio of speed 2 to speed 1. So we can follow this just as it's intended, which is to say that H2 to H1 is N2 to N1 quantity squared. So the head pressure goes with the square of the change in speed. That's an important thing to try to internalize and remember. And then the power, whether it's break horsepower or electrical power, which follows from break horsepower, goes with the cube of the change in speed. So even without looking at the affinity laws, I would suggest that those two ideas are kind of considered and internalized for you. In any case, we want to find H2. We know H1 and we know this ratio so we can get H2 by itself. H2 equals H1 times N2 over N1 quantity squared, where H1 is 150 feet and that ratio is now known to be 0.6, which is being squared, so it's going to be 0.36. So it's going to be less than half of 150 feet, not even 75 comes out to be 54 feet. Best answer choice is B. And now that you have that intuitive sense of the direction of change and like a sense of the magnitude of the change that it's going to go with the square and that it's going to reduce. You can eliminate C and D pretty quickly. And now if you had to flip a coin, you'd be going between A and B, even not doing any math. But the math here is pretty straightforward. So I think you can take this one through no matter what.
