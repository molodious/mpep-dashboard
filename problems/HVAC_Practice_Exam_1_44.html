Full Solution: The operating pressure in a hydronic system is 15 feet of head and the required flow is 120 GPM. The system has been designed with three pumps operating in parallel. Referring to the pump curves below, what is the minimum impeller size sufficient for the system? So the question is asking about the impeller size that's shown here on the left of the chart. We've got four choices. So in this four curves, we want to figure out what's the right one for the job. Now when you have pumps in parallel, the pressure that they generate, the head pressure is the same. And the volume flow rate is additive. So for every additional pump that we add in parallel, we get the additional flow rate contributed by that pump, but all of the pumps operate at the same delta P or the same head. So in this case, if the total required flow is 120 GPM, and we're going to be using three pumps to achieve that, then each individual pump only needs to generate one third of that. So we can say that our Q for any one pump is 120 GPM or three, which is 40 GPM. But the head is not a third of 15. It's the full 15 because each and every pump has to operate, making the full 15 feet. So we're going to find that point. We'll come horizontally over on the 15 foot line of the vertical axis and then vertically up from 40 on the horizontal axis and we identify this point here. That's our operating point that we want to select from. And we have to make a choice because we're in between four and a quarter and four and a half. And we should choose the larger one because we don't want to be too small. We'd rather be too big. So we don't have a shortfall. Best answer choice is C.
