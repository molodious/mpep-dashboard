Well, there's a fuel gas burner and a combustion intake fan. These are hints that it's burning hydrocarbons in order to make the heat that it uses to make hot water. So anytime we have a combustion reaction, we're burning hydrocarbons. We need air to be present in order to do that and at higher elevation, there is less oxygen present. There's less of everything present, right? What are the constituents of air? Nitrogen, oxygen, and water vapor. And when the density is lower, all the molecules are further spread out. And we can account for that by using something called the density factor.
